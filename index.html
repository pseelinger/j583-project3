The Unsheltered Homeless

It may seem a bit redunant to call homeless people "unsheltered", but the term refers to a specific set of people. The fact is, there are not enough shelters in the U.S. to house its large homeless population, even temporarily. Below is a graph of the percentage of a state's total homeless population that cannot find shelter. From 2007 to 2014, we've seen a slight decrease in these percentages overall, but some states have seen a significant increase in their unsheltered homeless populations.
