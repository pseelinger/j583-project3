The Unsheltered Homeless

A certain subset of homeless people in the United States falls into a category labeled "unsheletered". This refers to those individuals who meet the critria for homelessness, but are also unable to regularly find some sort of shelter at night, such as a homeless shelter. In most states, a large percentage of the homeless population falls into that category due to the fact that there are just simply not enought shelters to house the nation's large homeless population.

Below is a graph showing the percentage of each state's homeless population that is unsheltered. Use the drop down menu to compare states, or to compare a state against the national average. You can also hover over the points for each year to see the exact percentage. Clicking "Reset Graph" will clear all lines off the graph.
